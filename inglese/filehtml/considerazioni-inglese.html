I PINK FLOYD

consideration

The composition of Pink Floyd makes clear references to the emotions that each of us experiences daily, not only in relation to young people still on the path of education but rather to individuals who, having completed it, face life, bearing the weight of responsibilities, and preserving their independence.

Some manage to do so through a journey, exploring the world around them, while others rely on luck, and some seek comfort in substances that, unfortunately, only lead to further suffering.

This doesn't just refer to drugs, alcohol, and smoking, but also to everyday tools like phones or video games, which can become real addictions. These situations force those who are suffering to build a barrier around themselves, focusing more on the dependency and preventing others from offering help.

Addictions, along with other life issues like social pressures and failures, are, in my opinion, represented in the song through the scene of the feverish boy. The death of the brother, on the other hand, symbolizes the losses and pains from which no one can escape.

However, in my view, this represents life itself, which, despite its negative aspects, shapes us and makes us who we want to be.
