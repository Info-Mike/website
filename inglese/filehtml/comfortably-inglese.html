ANALYSIS OF THE SONG

The Wall

"The Wall" is a rock opera that narrates the story of Pink. Pink, a prominent figure in the world of rock, decides to withdraw into himself due to tragic events in his life, such as the death of his father during the war, overprotective mother, alienation from the entertainment world, and divorce from his wife. This decision is symbolized by the construction of a metaphorical wall around himself. After reflecting on the protagonist Pink's childhood and youth, he, under the control of his managers, is saved from an overdose only to be pushed onto the stage for yet another concert, highlighted in "Comfortably Numb."

The character of Pink serves as a device used by Waters to recount the significant period of instability the band was experiencing due to issues related to Barrett. The album is therefore considered autobiographical, setting it apart from the numerous compilations born during the same period.

Trama

Pink's return to the past, as narrated in the preceding songs, is abruptly interrupted to focus the scene on the moment when his manager barges into the hotel room. Here, a doctor administers a sedative to mitigate the effects of the drug, ensuring that Pink can still perform in a concert later that evening.

The doctor's words elicit a confused response from Pink, who recalls fragments of his childhood, linking them to his current situation, all while in a state of "Comfortably Numb."

descrizione del video della canzone

Each song of the piece was also accompanied by a short film aimed at representing the poignant themes of the songs through raw images that would make listeners reflect. In the case of "Comfortably Numb," a group of individuals consisting of Pink's manager, the hotel owner, and some paramedics burst into Pink's hotel room, finding him unconscious seated in an armchair in front of the television. The hotel owner shows no compassion for Pink, while his manager insists that "He's an artist," to ensure quick assistance. After injecting a drug into Pink's arm, the paramedics drag him into his limousine, ready to take him to the concert. During the journey, the drug causes Pink some hallucinations, portraying his body in a state of decay, falling and tearing as if it were a costume, ultimately revealing the protagonist in an outfit resembling that of a Nazista.



These scenes are interspersed with flashbacks depicting a young Pink finding a country mouse and showing it to his mother, who becomes frightened at the sight of it. This reaction prompts Pink to hide the mouse in a shed near his home. After a few days, during which Pink recovers from the flu, he returns to the small shed to find the mouse lifeless. Pink decides to throw it into a nearby river, further emphasizing the loneliness that Pink is enduring.

Composizione

One of the most significant songs in Pink's story is "Comfortably Numb." The duration of the song is approximately 6 minutes and 18 seconds, beginning with the simultaneous entrance of electric bass, drums, and synthesizer. The verses are structured to create a dialogue between Pink (performed by David Gilmour) and a doctor (Waters). The track opens with the doctor's voice, followed by two choruses sung by Gilmour.

The song features two electric guitar solos, both performed by Gilmour. The first solo, brief and melodic, follows the initial chorus, while the final solo (or outro) concludes the song with a gradual fade-out. This latter solo was ranked fourth among the "100 Greatest Guitar Solos" by Guitar World magazine and voted the best of all time by listeners of the British radio station Planet Rock in August 2006.
